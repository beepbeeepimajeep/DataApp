We are testing whether music students have a higher mean Math SAT score.

Hypotheses:  
\(H_0:\mu_1=\mu_2\)  
\(H_a:\mu_1>\mu_2\)

Test statistic:

\[
t=\frac{\bar x_1-\bar x_2}{\sqrt{\frac{s_1^2}{n_1}+\frac{s_2^2}{n_2}}}
\]

\[
t=\frac{581.667-523.818}{\sqrt{\frac{1156.75}{9}+\frac{1063.964}{11}}}
\approx 3.854
\]

Right-tailed test with \(\alpha=0.01\) and \(df=17\):

\[
t_c \approx 2.567
\]

Since \(3.854>2.567\), reject \(H_0\). There is sufficient evidence to support the claim.

\[
\boxed{3.854,\ 2.567,\ \text{Reject }H_0,\ \text{Yes}}
\]
