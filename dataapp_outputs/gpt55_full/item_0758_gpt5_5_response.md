The question asks whether averaging household sizes reported by randomly sampled individuals estimates the average household size across households.

Sampling individuals overrepresents larger households: a household of size 4 is four times as likely to contribute someone to the sample as a household of size 1. Therefore, the sample average estimates the household size of a randomly chosen individual, not a randomly chosen household.

So it is systematically biased upwards as an estimator of the average size of Vancouver households.
